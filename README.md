### "Resource Plundering" or Market and Efficiency Driven? — Motivations of China’s Direct Investment in Africa

**Authors:** Zhu Limeng, Han Yu

*(Jiangxi University of Finance and Economics, Nanchang 330013, China)*

---

### Abstract

China's Belt and Road Initiative has boosted trade and investment between China and Africa, causing Western unease and doubts of China's investment behavior in the region. Using a sample of 44 African countries that received Chinese investment between 2005 and 2020, this paper employs a system GMM model to examine the effects of host-country natural resource endowments, market size, and efficiency factors on China’s investment in Africa. It also divides African countries into low-income economies and middle-income-and-above economies to further analyze China’s investment motives across countries at different income levels. The results confirm the paper’s two hypotheses: China’s investment in Africa is jointly driven by host-country natural resources, market size, and efficiency factors; the effect of natural resources on China’s investment in Africa is limited and becomes progressively weaker as the economic level of African host countries rises. Resource-seeking is not the only motive behind China’s direct investment in Africa, and there is no “resource plundering”; market-seeking and efficiency-seeking are the decisive factors.
